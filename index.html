Hey, I'm

Leo Liao

I teach compilers to talk to AI — and sometimes the other way around.

I'm a senior computer scientist at Lawrence Livermore National Lab, where I've spent nearly two decades making parallel programs faster, safer, and less painful to write. These days I'm obsessed with getting large language models to actually understand code — not just autocomplete it, but reason about optimizations, catch race conditions, and translate between languages.

I built the ROSE compiler (R&D 100 Award winner), CompilerGPT, and a bunch of tools that live at the intersection of compilers and AI. When I'm not doing that, I'm writing about AI agents, governance, and the future of human-AI collaboration.

🔥 What I'm excited about right now

  • Building an open-source AI Co-Scientist — multi-agent research hypothesis generation
  • Exploring how AI agents can write entire books (88k words, 5 agents, 5 hours)
  • Making compilers explain themselves in plain English via LLMs
