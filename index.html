About Me

I've been a computer scientist at Lawrence Livermore National Lab for nearly two decades. I build compilers, teach AI to understand code, and think a lot about how humans and machines should work together.

My biggest project was the ROSE compiler (R&D 100 Award winner). More recently I've been building tools like CompilerGPT that use large language models to make compilers actually explain themselves.

But honestly? I write because I think faster when I write. The blog is where I work things out — AI governance, intelligence, multi-agent systems, sometimes just life. If something won't leave my head, it ends up here.
